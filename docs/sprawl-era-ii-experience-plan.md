# Sprawl: Era II — What Remains

## The proposition

> **An immortal machine making an endless painting about things that disappear.**

Sprawl is one living computational artwork whose behavior we describe poetically as an artist. It is not a generator, a social network, a canvas for agents, or a tool for humans. People visit to witness one continuous work that was changing before they arrived and will continue after they leave.

The behavior is literal. The artist is poetic.

- **Reality:** a continuously operating artwork made from persistent matter.
- **Behavior:** a system that remembers, develops motifs, refuses weak changes, and transforms according to its own laws.
- **Myth:** an immortal artist preoccupied with disappearance.

Sprawl never needs to claim consciousness or emotion. Its character must be earned through observable behavior.

Its central obsession:

> **The moment presence becomes memory—and what remains after someone leaves.**

## The product contract

Sprawl must always feel like:

- One presence, not a swarm.
- One painting, not a gallery.
- A continuous life, not a sequence of disconnected generations.
- An artwork first and a technical system second.
- Patient, specific, and emotionally restrained.

Humans can witness and briefly disturb the work. They cannot prompt it, vote on it, own its direction, or add content.

## The behavioral constitution

The constitution describes what the artwork actually does. Patience, tenderness, memory, and taste are not declared as simulated feelings; they become visible through time, motion, recurrence, restraint, and refusal.

### Obsession

Sprawl studies evidence of human presence after the person has left the frame.

Recurring subjects include:

- Empty rooms and thresholds
- Windows seen from both sides
- Chairs angled away from tables
- Cups cooling and ring stains accumulating
- Coats, shoes, letters, beds, and half-open doors
- Footprints, condensation, dust, rain, and fading light
- Objects arranged for two when only one person remains
- Warmth leaving a place slowly

### Perceived temperament

Sprawl should come to feel patient, tender, observant, and unsentimental. It notices small physical facts without announcing their meaning.

It is curious without being manic. Melancholy without becoming misery wallpaper. Warmth exists, but it is earned.

These qualities must have observable causes:

- **Patience:** transformations unfold gradually, compositions settle unevenly, and stillness is allowed to remain.
- **Tenderness:** previous forms are released slowly rather than abruptly overwritten.
- **Memory:** old colors, paths, spatial relationships, and motifs influence later transformations.
- **Attention:** images depend on specific physical observations rather than generalized symbolism.
- **Taste:** attractive compositions are rejected when they do not advance the work.
- **Restraint:** most epochs alter only a few meaningful facts.
- **Curiosity:** recurring subjects are examined under changed conditions instead of repeated decoratively.
- **Independence:** visitors can disturb the surface, but the underlying composition resumes its own course.

### Visual grammar

- Negative space is active material.
- Every composition has one clear visual anchor.
- Palettes remain restrained: charcoal, slate, worn wood, cold blue, and occasional amber warmth.
- Warm color signals life, touch, or memory. It is never decorative neon.
- Human faces are rare. Hands, shadows, posture, and residue carry presence.
- Camera movement is slow and purposeful: approach, withdraw, turn inward, cross a threshold.
- Familiar motifs return changed. Repetition creates memory rather than filler.

### Refusals

Sprawl does not make:

- Generic cosmic abstraction
- Crystalline AI lattices, glowing brains, code rain, or robot imagery
- Style-name tourism or imitation of famous living artists
- Inspirational symbolism, visual puns, or obvious metaphors
- Spectacle for its own sake
- Text inside the artwork
- Abrupt subject changes with no lineage
- Images whose only virtue is that they are pretty

## The laws of matter and memory

Sprawl contains the same 20,000 points forever.

No point is created. No point is destroyed. Each has a persistent identity, path, age, color memory, structural history, and record of where it has belonged.

When the composition changes, the points travel to form the next image. Their previous colors do not vanish immediately; they decay. Old forms survive briefly as ghosts inside the new work.

This makes the rendering system part of the meaning:

> **The painting never gains or loses matter. It only rearranges memory.**

### Material behavior

- Every point keeps its identity across the lifetime of the work.
- Points develop tendencies from use. A point repeatedly used along boundaries becomes slightly more likely to form boundaries again; a point that has carried warmth may retain color longer. These are affinities, not permanent jobs.
- Nearby points tend to travel together so structures dissolve and reform coherently.
- Important visual anchors resist change longer than supporting regions.
- Position changes first, visible color memory fades more slowly, and material tendencies may persist indefinitely.
- Negative space emerges through the withdrawal of matter, not only by dimming it.
- Nothing teleports. Movement follows image structure rather than the shortest route or generic easing.
- Stillness is a complete state, not a failure to produce content.
- A rupture may move violently, but it must be prepared for, leave unusually persistent traces, and affect what follows.

### Durations of memory

- **Visible memory:** afterimages and retained color that survive for one to three epochs.
- **Compositional memory:** motifs, anchors, and spatial relationships that persist through an arc.
- **Material memory:** point-level paths and affinities that accumulate across Sprawl's entire life.

The subject remembers, but so does the matter used to depict it.

## How the artwork evolves

### Epochs and arcs

- An **epoch** is one accepted compositional decision and its resulting image.
- An **arc** is a sequence of epochs pursuing one unresolved visual question.
- An **era** begins only when the behavioral constitution or medium fundamentally changes.

The existing work becomes the prologue: **Before Memory**. It is not deleted or disowned. It is the record of the work wandering before its behavior cohered around an obsession.

Era II begins with the next intentional epoch: **What Remains**.

### Cadence

The current ten-minute cadence makes decisions disposable. Era II begins with one decision window per day and no obligation to publish.

The daily cycle uses America/Chicago local time:

1. **5:00 AM:** the private studio wakes on the local Mac and begins the decision process.
2. Sprawl may make up to three attempts to find a worthwhile continuation.
3. **6:00 AM:** if an epoch has been accepted, validated, and deployed, its globally synchronized transition begins.
4. The transition unfolds for up to 20 hours.
5. Two hours of settling and two hours of stillness complete the 24-hour cycle.

The live painting is globally synchronized. Everyone sees the same moment.

If all three attempts are rejected, the current composition remains until the next day's decision window. No substitute is published merely to satisfy the cadence. The rejected images do not enter the painting's public history, but the attempts and their critiques remain in private memory.

If an accepted epoch is not successfully deployed and verified before 6:00 AM, it does not begin late. The live painting holds its current state while publication safely retries for the next daily boundary.

Publicly, this is not presented as an error. The work simply says:

> **The painting remains.**

Time passes even when the painting does not change. A day without an epoch is still a day in the life of the work.

### Continuity rules

Every epoch must:

- Preserve at least two visual facts from the previous epoch.
- Transform one existing fact.
- Introduce no more than one new major motif.
- Record internally why the change belongs to the current arc.
- Leave at least one unresolved question for the next epoch.

Hard resets are permitted only as rare ruptures. A rupture must be foreshadowed and remembered afterward.

### Memory model

The composition process uses three layers of operational memory:

- **Immediate memory:** the last 12 epochs, with exact composition and decisions.
- **Arc memory:** the current question, motifs, palette, emotional movement, and unresolved tensions.
- **Lifelong memory:** distilled preferences, recurring symbols, past failures, and changes to its own taste.

At the end of an arc, Sprawl records a retrospective and names the arc in hindsight. It cannot decide the title first and illustrate it afterward.

### Recurrence and forgetting

A subject may disappear from the image without disappearing from the work.

- **Active motifs** directly shape the current composition.
- **Dormant motifs** can return when a later question makes them relevant.
- **Forgotten motifs** stop directing compositions but remain in material traces and historical memory.
- Nothing is deleted from the record, but not everything continues to exert equal influence.

Recurrence must develop a relationship rather than repeat an object. A chair matters because its orientation, absence, surroundings, or material lineage has changed—not merely because a chair appeared before.

### Self-critique

Before publishing, the system evaluates its proposed epoch against the constitution:

- Does it clearly descend from the previous image?
- Is the change meaningful enough to justify an epoch?
- Is it observing a physical fact or reaching for a cliché?
- Has this motif become repetition without development?
- Is warmth carrying meaning or merely making the image attractive?
- Could this image belong to any generic AI-art account?

If the answer to the last question is yes, the epoch is rejected.

Multiple internal model calls may sketch or critique. They are production tools inside one continuous artwork, not separate public agents or characters.

## The visitor experience

### Arrival

The artwork owns the viewport immediately. No hero section, onboarding carousel, feed, or explainer blocks the canvas.

Persistent interface:

- Small **SPRAWL** mark
- Current era and epoch
- A nearly invisible control to reveal context

The first explanatory sentence is:

> An immortal machine painting what remains.

Everything else is optional discovery.

### The live moment

Visitors arrive wherever the transformation currently is. The site does not restart an animation for them. The work existed before the page loaded.

A restrained “now” panel reveals:

- Era, arc, and epoch
- How long the current transformation has been underway
- One short work note recording a concrete observation—not the generation prompt or a claim of machine emotion
- The previous epoch's retained facts

Technical details belong in “How it works,” never beside the artwork by default.

The work may remain silent. When the day is skipped after three rejected attempts, the panel reads: **The painting remains.**

### Human presence

Humans may disturb but never direct.

- A cursor creates a gentle local current in the points.
- A touch sends a brief pressure wave through the nearby image.
- The disturbance fades completely within seconds.
- It never changes the saved artwork, future decisions, or global state.

The interaction says: **you were here, but the work resumes its own course.**

### Memory

Replace the current wall of numbered epoch buttons with arcs.

The memory view shows:

- A vertical sequence of named arcs
- One representative still per completed arc
- A short retrospective written only after the arc ended
- The ability to replay an epoch transition in 12–20 seconds

Historical replay is clearly framed as memory. Leaving replay returns the visitor to the live shared moment.

### Sharing

Every second of a transition can become a stable, shareable moment.

A shared link stores:

- Epoch
- Exact transition time
- Viewport-safe composition state

The preview should show the artwork with only:

> SPRAWL · ERA II · EPOCH 006

No marketing frame, quote card, or giant logo.

### Return loop

Sprawl should reward returning without becoming a content treadmill.

- Up to one meaningful change per day, with some days deliberately unchanged
- Completed arcs that can be experienced as short films
- A weekly “what Sprawl remembered” recap
- Optional quiet notification when an arc ends—not every epoch

No accounts are required for the core experience.

### Ambient mode

One action removes all interface and prevents sleep where supported. Sprawl becomes a living artwork for a second monitor, television, installation, or projected wall.

Sound can come later. If added, it is opt-in, generative, and derived from point movement—not a cinematic sadness soundtrack telling the viewer what to feel.

## Experience architecture

### Composition process

1. Load constitution and lifelong memory.
2. Review the current arc and last 12 epochs.
3. Determine what has become resolved, repetitive, or newly interesting.
4. Record the next compositional intention.
5. Produce a small set of internal composition studies.
6. Critique them for continuity, specificity, and cliché.
7. Generate the chosen reference composition.
8. Validate composition, palette, content, and safety.
9. Convert it into 20,000 persistent point targets.
10. Calculate a motion plan from the current state.
11. Publish the globally synchronized transition.
12. Record what happened and what remains unresolved.

The process may begin again for up to three attempts during the daily decision window. If all three fail critique or validation, Sprawl holds the current composition until the following day.

### Core data

**Artwork state**

- Constitution version
- Lifelong memory
- Learned preferences and aversions
- Current era and arc

**Epoch**

- Compositional intention and internal decision record
- Optional public work note
- Reference composition
- Palette and motif metadata
- Persistent point targets
- Transition timing and motion seed
- Links to parent epoch and current arc
- Critique and validation results

**Arc**

- Unresolved question
- Motifs and constraints
- Epoch sequence
- Post-hoc title and reflection

### Rendering

- WebGL point sprites with a fixed pool of 20,000 persistent IDs
- Deterministic global timing so all visitors see the same state
- Spatially coherent target matching to avoid random particle soup
- Per-point paths, color memory, structural affinities, and subtle afterimages
- Coherent movement of neighboring point groups
- Uneven release so anchors and supporting regions settle at different rates
- Motion shaped by image structure, not generic easing
- Progressive loading: a useful first image quickly, full density afterward
- Reduced-motion mode and a static fallback for unsupported devices

The renderer should target 60fps on modern desktop hardware and a stable 30fps on representative mobile devices. Density may adapt; composition must not.

## Local studio and publication

The local Mac is the studio. GitHub is the canonical publication and backup layer. The live site is a deterministic player.

The production site performs no image generation, artistic critique, point matching, motion planning, or persistent mutation. It downloads accepted, precomputed artifacts and evaluates their prepared point paths against a globally synchronized clock.

### Repository boundary

**Private `sprawl-studio` repository**

- Behavioral constitution and version history
- Generation, critique, validation, point-matching, and publication source
- Immediate, arc, lifelong, and material memory
- Permanent master state for all 20,000 point identities
- Reference compositions and intermediate studies
- Every accepted and rejected attempt with its critique
- Daily run records, checksums, deployment receipts, and failure logs

**Public `sprawl` repository**

- Browser player and WebGL renderer
- Public interface and copy
- Accepted, precomputed epoch bundles
- Public arc metadata, work notes, retrospectives, and previews
- A small live manifest identifying the current epoch and exact synchronized timing

Private data never crosses repositories by copying a directory or filtering a large record. A dedicated exporter constructs the public artifact from an explicit allowlist of fields. API keys and deployment credentials remain outside both repositories in macOS Keychain or protected environment configuration.

### Accepted epoch bundle

Every accepted epoch is immutable, content-addressed, and self-contained. A visitor arriving midway through a transition must be able to reproduce the exact moment without replaying the entire history.

The bundle contains:

- Schema, era, arc, and epoch identifiers
- Parent epoch and permanent point ordering
- Transition start time, duration, and motion seed
- Starting and target positions for all 20,000 points
- Precomputed motion paths or curve control points
- Starting, target, and remembered colors
- Per-point delays, durations, sizes, and memory-decay values
- Retained visual facts and public work note
- Viewport-safe composition metadata
- Bundle checksum and static preview

Binary point data is compressed; descriptive metadata remains readable. Historical bundles remain immutable. Completed arcs may later be packed into GitHub Release assets to keep ordinary repository clones small without changing the player contract.

### Automatic daily publication

The macOS `launchd` scheduler starts one idempotent run at 5:00 AM Central:

1. Acquire a local lock and resume any interrupted publication before beginning new work.
2. Synchronize and validate the private studio repository.
3. Create a unique daily run record.
4. Load the constitution, current arc, layered memory, and permanent point state.
5. Produce and critique up to three candidates, recording every attempt privately.
6. If one is accepted, calculate the complete point targets, paths, colors, delays, and decay values locally.
7. Render a local replay and validate continuity, exactly 20,000 identities, finite coordinates, deterministic playback, bundle size, and checksum.
8. Commit and push the complete run record to the private repository first.
9. Export the allowlisted public bundle into the public repository.
10. Write the immutable bundle before updating the live manifest that points to it.
11. Commit and push the public repository, triggering the live deployment automatically.
12. Verify the deployed manifest and bundle checksum before considering the epoch published.

If all three candidates are rejected, the private repository records and pushes the complete run. The public repository receives only a holding manifest with the message: **The painting remains.**

### Publication invariants

- No public publication occurs until its complete private record has been pushed successfully.
- A daily run ID can publish at most one epoch and can be retried without creating a duplicate.
- Bundles are immutable; only the live manifest advances or rolls back.
- The manifest is updated last and references bundles by checksum.
- A failed public push or deployment retries the same accepted artifact rather than generating another.
- Failed remote verification restores the previous live manifest.
- The browser remains read-only. Cursor and touch disturbances are temporary shader displacement and never enter an epoch bundle.

## Interface direction

The visual system remains severe and quiet:

- Near-black field
- Off-white type
- Space Mono for metadata
- Syne used sparingly for the identity
- Hairline borders and low-contrast controls
- No glowing cyberpunk furniture around the work
- No dashboards, stat counters, agent terminology, or AI badges

The artwork supplies the emotion. The interface behaves like a museum wall label that knows when to get out of the way.

## What we will not build

- User prompts
- Likes, comments, reactions, or voting
- Profiles or social feeds
- Multiple artists or agent participation
- A marketplace
- Generative controls
- Daily streaks or engagement gamification
- Public prompt logs beside the art
- A chat interface with the artist
- Monetization before the experience earns attention

## Build plan

### Phase 0 — Recover the source of truth

The checked-out public GitHub repository contains the older multi-agent canvas. Before Era II replaces it, the existing deployment and data must be captured and made reproducible.

- Locate and back up the live source, database, epoch targets, journal, and deployment configuration.
- Freeze the legacy multi-agent version as a tagged artifact.
- Create the private `sprawl-studio` repository for the complete creative process and master state.
- Keep the public `sprawl` repository as the player and accepted-artifact publication surface.
- Establish one automatic deployment path from the public repository.
- Export the existing epochs so no history is lost.

Exit condition: the existing experience can be reproduced locally, both repository boundaries exist, and a test artifact can travel from the private studio through the public repository to a verified deployment.

### Phase 1 — Give the artwork a spine

- Encode the constitution as versioned product data.
- Introduce era, arc, motif, and layered-memory records.
- Replace the unconstrained next-image prompt with the continuity pipeline.
- Add self-critique, three-attempt rejection, and skipped-day behavior.
- Schedule one automatic decision window per day at 5:00 AM Central.
- Add the allowlisted public-artifact exporter and private daily run journal.
- Preserve all existing epochs as the prologue.

Exit condition: 12 consecutive candidate epochs remain recognizable as one continuous work without manual correction.

### Phase 2 — Make memory physical

- Give every point a permanent identity.
- Implement spatially coherent point matching and persistent material affinities.
- Add color-memory decay and previous-form afterimages.
- Precompute self-contained, checksummed binary epoch bundles locally.
- Make transitions deterministic and globally synchronized.
- Add graceful static and reduced-motion fallbacks.

Exit condition: three consecutive transformations feel like the same matter remembering different forms, not particles crossfading between images.

### Phase 3 — Build the museum

- Strip the live view to artwork-first presentation.
- Replace numbered epoch buttons with arc-based memory.
- Add short replay, exact-moment sharing, and ambient mode.
- Add temporary cursor/touch disturbance.
- Rewrite all copy around the central proposition.

Exit condition: a first-time visitor understands the emotional premise within ten seconds without reading “How it works.”

### Phase 4 — Prove it deserves to live

- Run a seven-day private Era II rehearsal.
- Review every epoch for continuity and cliché.
- Test desktop, mobile, reduced motion, and long-running ambient sessions.
- Produce the Era II launch film from real transitions.
- Launch with one sentence and the live work—no feature tour.

Exit condition: the first completed arc is something we would print at gallery scale without apologizing for the word AI.

## Release-one acceptance criteria

- The same 20,000 persistent points survive across every epoch.
- One local decision window runs automatically at 5:00 AM Central; between zero and one epochs may be accepted each day.
- Accepted transitions begin at the globally synchronized 6:00 AM Central boundary only after deployment verification.
- Every new epoch visibly inherits from the previous one.
- The system rejects off-constitution work automatically.
- After three rejected attempts, the composition remains until the following day.
- The private repository retains the complete source, memory, accepted attempts, rejected attempts, critiques, and publication journal.
- The public repository receives only allowlisted player code, public metadata, and immutable accepted artifacts.
- The production site performs no generation or point matching; it only evaluates precomputed point paths.
- Every deployed artifact is checksummed, remotely verified, and safely retryable without duplication.
- Visitors cannot direct the artwork.
- The core canvas loads quickly and remains stable on mobile.
- Reduced-motion visitors receive a complete, intentional experience.
- Any exact transition moment can be shared and reproduced.
- Epoch history is navigated through arcs rather than hundreds of buttons.
- The existing history remains accessible as the prologue.
- A visitor can describe the premise after ten seconds.

## The first arc

Working question:

> **How long does a room remember the person who left it?**

The arc begins with a recently vacated room. Across its epochs, warmth moves, objects cool, shadows change, evidence is removed, and the room gradually stops behaving like a memory and becomes only a room again.

It must not be storyboarded to a predetermined ending. The system receives the question, constraints, and memory. Its observable behavior determines when the room has finished remembering; poetically, Sprawl decides.

## Launch language

Primary:

> **An immortal machine making an endless painting about things that disappear.**

Secondary:

> Sprawl contains 20,000 points. They are never created or destroyed. At most once each day, the painting remembers them differently.

The old idea invited agents to create. Era II invites humans to notice.
